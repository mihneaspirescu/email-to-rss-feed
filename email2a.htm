Argentina · Regulation · Operators

Decree that will contain more obligations to share infrastructure expected

The competitors of Telecom-Cablevisión await new regulations and trust that in addition to enabling pay TV -including DTH- in localities with more than 80,000 inhabitants, it would also define new obligations for the sharing of passive infrastructure and grant powers to Enacom (National Communications Entity) to resolve disputes between the parties. In addition, it would establish facilities for the deployment of radio bases. With fewer expectations, operators also demand measures to regulate the wholesale price market in the supply of relevant content, such as football.

Argentina · Data Center · E-Government

Cybersecurity Committee is migrating information from several areas of government to the Arsat data center

The Minister of Modernization, Andrés Ibarra, commented yesterday to Convergencialatina that the committee met last week, with representation from five ministries (two more than originally planned): Defense, Security, Modernization, Foreign Affairs and Justice. "We are working on data protection and migration of data centers from other areas of government, to the Arsat data center that we manage, to protect e-government information," Ibarra said in the framework of the GSMA event on Digital Economy, carried out in parallel to the WTO Ministerial Conference.

Source: A Diario

Argentina · Regulation · Operators

Cablevision-Telecom mega-merger to be approved before the end of the year

The Minister of Modernization, Andrés Ibarra, confirmed that the Enacom (National Communications Agency) will approve the mega-merger of Cablevisión with Telecom, probably on December 21st. He announced it in the framework of the Digital Economy Forum organized by GSMA and Cepal. The official also explained that today there is a ceiling of 140 MHz per operator and new spectrum will be tendered to extend it but they are evaluating in which bands and the process of cleaning the spectrum.

Ecuador · Economy

The fate of electronic money is in hands of the Assembly, which has a month to decide

The bill Economic Reactivation Law has been submitted by the Executive on November 1 and the Assembly approved it 28 days later with more than 65% of modifications to the initial content, and now continues  in the hands of the Central Bank of Ecuador (BCE, for its Spanish acronym). President Lenin Moreno partially vetoed the Law, reaffirming his decision that electronic money should go to Private Banking. Now the Legislature has 30 days to resolve whether or not to give it a partial veto.

Globales · Pay TV

Disney to buy Foxs international assets of films and TV for US$ 52.4 billion

These assets include the studios that produce the megaproductions of Marvel superheroes, the "Avatar" franchise and television shows as "The Simpsons." Thanks to Fox's participation in the Hulu video streaming service, Disney will take on the majority control of one of Netflix's main competitors.

Fox shareholders will receive 0.2745 shares of Disney for each paper they own, at US$ 29.50 per share, according to Reuters calculations.

Prior to carring out the acquisition, Fox will separate the network and stations of Fox Broadcasting, Fox News Channel, Fox Business Network, FS1, FS2 and Big Ten Network in a company recently listed on the stock market that will be split to its shareholders. The television stations of 21st Century Fox and the Fox News channel will also be separated.

As part of the agreement, Disney will assume around US$ 13.7 billion in 21st Century Fox net debt.

Source: El Economista - México | Original article

Perú · Internet & OTT

Technological solution for the preservation of Cultural Heritage presented

Smart Patrimonio, developed by Telefónica, is based on the installation of small devices, with high sensorization capabilities and different functionalities, in heritage sites and properties, which allows one to control structural and environmental parameters remotely in real time, automate some conservation tasks and predict critical situations. Thus, preventive and maintenance strategies are established with the objective of conserving infrastructure and goods in the best conditions. The solution has already been implemented in Europe and Chile.

Uruguay · Politics

Uruguay asks that the FTA with Europe respect national regulations on telecommunications

This was stated by the general director of the secretariat of the Ministry of Industry, Energy and Mining (MIEM, for its Spanish acronym), Fernanda Cardona, in relation to negotiations for the signing of a Free Trade Agreement (FTA) between Mercosur and the European Union (EU).

She explained that in this case the chapter on telecommunications could not be excluded from negotiations, as was the case of the FTA between Uruguay and Chile, because it is a negotiation between blocks. "In the case of the negotiation with Chile, Uruguay transferred its interest and managed to exempt telecommunications, a situation that cannot be applied in the case of Mercosur-EU, because the interest of Uruguay is not shared by the rest of Mercosur," said Cardona. The official noted "the complexity of negotiating first between the region and then with the other bloc or region, in this case with the EU, so that Uruguay cannot always achieve its particular interests."

Source: La Diaria
