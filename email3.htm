Argentina · Satellites · Spectrum

Arsat to review wholesale access prices

The president of Arsat, Rodrigo De Loredo, affirmed that they will review the flat rate of US$ 18 per mega wholesaler and that "there will soon be news about it". During the Cappi National Meeting that took place this week, the official said that there are currently 355 localities connected to the Federal Internet Plan and that it continues advancing although with difficulties: "We have the funds but sometimes we fall behind because it is difficult to deploy in some locations "due to municipal permits, he justified.

Argentina · Software and Applications · Regulation

Argentina adheres to the Budapest Convention to fight against cybercrime

The Chamber of Deputies gave sanction to the law that adheres Argentina to the Budapest Convention with the aim of fighting against cybercrime. The initiative had already received a sanction in Senators in April. The law will give tools to the Justice to investigate crimes on the Web and is the only international instrument in force on cybercrime and digital evidence.

Bolivia · Operators

Entels fiber optic service reached 53 populations last June

Between the months of June and November, the optical fiber of the National Telecommunications Company (Entel, for its Spanish acronym) reached 53 towns in various parts of the country. With this new service, the state company benefited more than 7,000 customers.

In the department of Santa Cruz it benefited 15 locations, in Beni 11, Cochabamba 6, La Paz 5, Potosí 5, Chuquisaca 4, Tarija 4, Oruro 2 and Pando 1, according to the company.

The General Manager of Entel, Óscar Coca, assured that the service is competitive in relation to other operators because it has financial backing that does not require subsidies, besides the increases of the Internet speed and does not register cuts.

Source: Cambio - Bolivia | Original article

Brasil · E-Government

Government launches program to bring high-speed broadband to 22,000 schools

This is the Connected Education project that seeks to bring high-capacity broadband to all public schools in the country through 2024. The initial investment - up to December 2018 - is of US$ 84 million and will be used to connect at least 22,400 schools, which house more than 12 million students, according to the Ministry of Education.

The connectivity will be carried out with the capacity of the Geostationary Defense and Communications Satellite (SGDC, for its Portuguese acronym) to reach remote locations or those where optical fiber does not arrive. While the terrestrial part will be supplied by the National Research and Education Network  (RPN, for its Portuguese acronym) and the Amazonia Conectada and Northeast Conectado programs. To bring broadband to schools, the Ministry of Education will use the capacity of the Brazilian geostationary satellite (SGDC, for its Portuguese acronym) to connect remote and hard-to-install fiber optic sites. Terrestrial Internet will be provided by the RPN and by the Amazonia Conectada and Northeast Conectado programs. The speeds range from 10 Mbps to 100 Mbps, with quality monitored by the Internet Management Committee in Brazil (CGI.br, for its Portuguese acronym).

Source: Teletime

Brasil · Operators

Plan approved by Ois council could prevent the entry of China Telecom

The problem for the entry of China Telecom is the limit of capital increase established in the proposal, which foresees up to US$ 2.5 billion, of which between US$ 1.1 billion and US$ 1.7 billion can be subscribed by creditors, and US$ 775 million subscribed by shareholders. The issuance of convertible debentures in shares for a total value of US$ 930 million is also foreseen.

According to Teletime, sources linked to the agreement between the government and China Telecom would indicate that these conditions would prevent the Chinese company from entering Oi through a public offering of shares with the amount it wanted. On the contrary, it would have to negotiate with controlling shareholders and creditors. And the control of the company, an essential condition for the entry of China Telecom, would have a higher price than what was expected.

Source: Teletime

Brasil · Mobile

92% of households have a cell phone, but only 66% have sewer treatment

According to data from Pnad Continua, disseminated by the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE, for its Portuguese acronym), access to mobile technology advances faster than the universalization of basic sanitation. In 2016, while 92.3% or 63.8 million Brazilian households had at least one resident with a cell phone, only 66% or 45.6 million families had wastewater treatment.

Chile · Free to Air TV - TDT

TVNs capitalization to be discussed in Deputies next week

Next week the Science and Technology Committee of the Lower House will be the instance in which TVNs capitalization project for US$ 47 million will once again be discussed, after its approval in the Senate. For that day, the Minister General Secretary of Government, Paula Narváez, and the Chairman of the Board of TVN, Ricardo Solari have been invited to speak before the Science and Technology Commission.

Once that review is finished, the initiative will go to deputies. From the Chamber officials commented that the processing would be expedited. This, because the Government would have committed the necessary votes in a similar way to what happened in the Senate. In that instance, the injection of capital was approved by 19 votes in favor and 7 against.

Chile · Operators

Problems in Entels service due to cuts in its optical fiber

Several reports from Entel users warned of a problem in the company's signal in its mobile Internet and voice telephone services, a situation that affected not only the capital, but multiple cities in the country. Those who were also affected by this situation would be WOM customers, because the company uses the same antennas as Entel.

In addition, the Undersecretariat of Telecommunications (Subtel, for its Spanish acronym) pointed out regarding the failure of Entels mobile services for voice and data that the incidence was presented due to a fiber optic cut registered between San Fernando and Curicó, which the company tried to resolve, but resulted in congestion, causing problems in the continuity of service in areas I, II, III, IV, V, VII, VIII, IX, X and XI, as well as in the Metropolitan Region.

Chile · Regulation

GSMA presents its recommendations to modernize the regulatory system

The GSMA has launched a new report called "Keys to the Modernisation of Digital Ecosystem Regulation in Chile," which analyzes the impact of the "Fourth Industrial Revolution" and the digital economy on existing regulation, which is increasingly lagging behind.

"The institutional framework that regulates ICTs needs an urgent review, if Chile intends to maintain its indicators of regional leadership," said Sebastián Cabello, Regional Director of the GSMA for Latin America. "For example, the creation of an organization that can act on the entire digital ecosystem to address the dynamism of the current market and effectively circumvent the cross-cutting nature of digital issues that are now being addressed in a very fragmented manner by different government agencies," he added.

Colombia · Regulation

CRC publishes the Road Map for the digital economy

The roadmap had been presented by the regulator in Andicom this year and identifies the actions and projects that must be addressed by government entities of the province of La Paz, under the current institutional framework. The commissioner of the Communications Regulation Commission (CRC), Germán Bacca Medina, anticipated to Convergencia  that the road map distinguishes three types of actions: sectoral, regulatory and transversal to all sectors, and that the regulators objective is to locate other regulators, alien to ICTs, as a technical advisor, which today face the entrance of new actors, such as Uber in transport and Airbnb in hotels, among others.

Irregularities in the election of SIGETs private representative reported

The National Association of Private Enterprise (ANEP, for its Spanish acronym) noted that Sánchez Trejo, appointed second director of the General Superintendence of Electricity and Telecommunications (SIGET, for its Spanish acronym), has a conflict of interest because he is also president of the Federation of Bar Associations of El Salvador (Fedaes, for its Spanish acronym). According to Luis Cardenal, president of the ANEP, "the election of Sánchez Trejo as the second director of the SIGET was vitiated by government intervention and a series of anomalies that make it illegitimate. El Salvador · Smartphones & Devices

Almost 90% of Salvadorans use a smartphone

According to the survey carried out by the Salvadoran Public Opinion Research Center (CIOPS, for its Spanish acronym), 89.7% of the population uses a smart phone. The report also revealed that 41.7% have a desktop computer and 56.9% have a laptop. As for social networks, the preferred in the country is still Facebook, followed by Instagram.

México · Regulation

For the National Regeneration Movement (Morena), Mexico must move away from the Trump model of Internet regulation

Zoe Robledo, senator of the leftist National Regeneration Movement (Morena, for its Spanish acronym), argued that Internet providers "cannot and should not decide based on a criterion of economic efficiency only, what content they reward, punish, directly or indirectly prevent download or enjoy." Robledo stressed that the regulator has a challenge: safeguard the digital rights that are enunciated in the Constitution and at the same time enable the organic growth of broadband connectivity while preserving the fundamental values of the network.

Source: El Universal - México | Original article

México · Regulation

Telephone numbering will go to 10 digits in 2019

The Federal Telecommunications Institute informed that as of August 3, 2019, it will have 10 digits. The 01 will be eliminated for calls to non-geographic numbers and national long distance calls; and 044 for calls from fixed lines destined to National Mobile Numbers in "The caller pays" model.

Perú · Politics

Project to create the ICT Vice Ministry is in Congress

The Executive Branch has sent to Congress a bill to create the Vice Ministry of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT), within the Ministry of Transport and Communications (MTC). The initiative proposes to reformulate the Vice Ministry of Communications and turn it into the Vice Ministry of Information and Communication Technologies, which will have competence, in a shared manner with regional and local governments, in infrastructure and telecommunications and ICT services for the private sector, society civil and the academic field.
