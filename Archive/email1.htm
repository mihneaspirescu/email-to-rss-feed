Argentina · Mobile

Catel goes for the national 4Play and Telefónica points to the fiber of cooperatives

The president of the Chamber of Telecommunications Cooperatives (Catel), Ariel Fernández Alvarado, explained that the Chamber will manage 80% of the value chain of the Mobile Virtual Operator (MVNO) that it will launch in partnership with Telefónica, including the management of the user and of the business. The cooperatives will provide a single national brand and will offer quadruple play packages to 500,000 households and 2 million potential customers. In the first stage they will seek, which will be launched in mid-2018, to attract some 300,000 subscribers, said the official to Convergencialatina. They will compete directly with Cablevisión-Personal because they have a television base that Movistar or Claro do not have. They also ratified negotiations with Colsecor to add contents with an OTT.

Catel will provide a centralized platform that will allow the operation with its own infrastructure to its associates through the investment of more than US$ 2 million. Alvarado highlighted the advantage that can be achieved with the increase of offload traffic through the fixed networks of cooperatives.

On the Telefónica agreement, it will also include the commercialization of handsets. Luis Delamer, director of regulation and wholesaler of Telefónica, clarified that the agreement does not have ramifications but recognized the companys intention in advancing through associativity to access physical infrastructure. The executive considered that there is a predisposition to talk about the possibility of accessing the fixed networks of cooperatives where Telefónica does not have deployments.

Source: Convergencialatina

State looks for alternatives to get rid of its participation in Telefónica

Colombia · Spectrum

ANE brought connectivity to coffee growers with the use of white spaces spectrum

Paola Herrera, deputy director of management and technical planning at the National Spectrum Agency (ANE), explained that the use of this technology allows them to connect remote populations less than 10,000 inhabitants. "Where the spectrum is not being used, it can be implemented for other things. White spaces  are normally enabled in television spectrum. In the territories, 49 channels are available for this service and, since it is unlikely that several producers will arrive to the municipalities, there are still many free channels," explained Herrera.

Mesetas is the first municipality to use this technology and to connect the inhabitants of Vereda La Guajira, it was necessary to implement special devices that are imported from the United States and Europe. For the implementation of white spaces it was necessary to use a telecommunications tower, together with antennas, solar panels and fiber optic points, infrastructure that was lent by Anditel.

Source: El Tiempo - Colombia | Original article

Colombia · Spectrum

Asomóvil claims auction of the 700 MHz band to MinTic

According to the Association of Mobile Operators (Asomóvil, for its Spanish acornym), the Ministry of Information Technology and Communications (MinTic) must invest more and start the 700 MHz auction as soon as possible. According to the Alcatel study, "Mobile Economy Latin America and the Caribbean 2017 "on connectivity in Colombia, 46% of the population still connects in 2G, 39% in 3G and 15% in 4G. The association calls for greater investment from MinTic and claims the need to "(...) reactivate the industry, telecommunications has had a negative growth in the last eight quarters and that slows down private sector investments."

México · Economy

NAFTA: Mexico expects an e-commerce boom from new regulations

According to experts, the new rules would give certainty and protection to e-commerce; in addition boost this activity with measures such as the increase in the general value of shipping the goods that can be imported by parcel or electronic commerce. It is expected that the digital commerce chapter of the NAFTA will be closed or could almost be finalized when it is discussed this Thursday and Friday. This chapter should include the so-called safe harbor provisions on the responsibility of the Internet service provider, according to Canada's chief negotiator, Steve Verheul.

México · Fixed Broadband · Regulation

Regulator puts the minimum speeds for Internet to public consultation

Until February 19, those interested will be able to comment on the preliminary draft that sets minimum broadband parameters to which the service providers should be subject. The text proposes that mobile broadband speeds should be equal to or greater than 1 Mbps upload and 4 Mbps download in 2018. While minimum fixed broadband speeds should be 1 Mbps upload and 10 Mbps download.

México · Regulation

Countries that renegotiate the NAFTA agree to ban monopolies in telecommunications

In a new round of negotiation of the telecommunications chapter of the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) between the United States, Canada and Mexico, the nations agreed to ban monopolies in the sector and recognized as a fact that the Federal Telecommunications Institute (IFT, for its Spanish acronym) can determine interconnection rates of the sector, and not the Mexican Congress. A source familiar with the negotiations reported that the new chapter in this area includes regulations on mobile telecommunications and international roaming charges, while it allows companies to transmit data across borders.

Source: El Economista - México | Original article

Paraguay · Internet & OTT

Electronic transfers increased 21.7% between January and September

Electronic transfers between entities of the financial system registered an increase in volume and value. Last September these operations increased 21.7% in volume and 8.6% in values, in year-on-year terms, according to the "Financial Stability" report of the Central Bank of Paraguay (BCP, for its Spanish acronym). Likewise, the transfer between customers of financial institutions also grew in volume and amount. Between January and September, 1.420,109 transactions were recorded, which represented an increase of 94% in the number of operations and 31.6% in the amount, compared to the same period of the previous year.

Source: ABC - Paraguay | Original article

Perú · Terrestrial Backbones

Movistar and Bitel did not submit proposals for regional networks and Claro left the tender

Pro Inversión received envelopes two and three of the bidding processes of the regional networks for Amazonas, Ica and Lima, on the one hand, and Junín, Puno, Tacna and Moquegua, on the other. The process, whose winner of the good pro will be known on Monday 18, includes operating and maintaining Internet access networks for an average of ten years. For the first package, of the 10 prequalified bidders, four were present: Grupo Integración Tecnológica del Perú, América Móvil (Claro), Gilat Perú y Consorcio GMC Conecta, although not all  have applied for the three regions. Telefónica, Bitel, Consorcio Perú rural, Consorcio Satelital Coprimi and Consorcio Smart Time Perú did not show up.For the second group of regional networks neither América Móvil (Claro) nor Movistar nor Bitel participated. Those that participated are: Consorcio de Integración Tecnológica del Perú, Gilat, Consorcio de Telecomunicaciones Rurales del Perú, Consorcio GMC Conecta and Consorcio Bandtel. Of these, only Gilat and Consorcio Telecomunicaciones Rurales del Peru applied for the four regions.

Source: Convergencialatina

Regional · Regulation

Regulators of Peru and the Dominican Republic sign a cooperation agreement

With the agreement signed by the Supervisory Agency of Private Investment in Telecommunications (Osiptel, for its Spanish acronym) and the Dominican Telecommunications Institute of the Dominican Republic (Indotel, for its Spanish acronym), created a framework of technical and institutional cooperation for the development of telecommunications in both countries, through the execution of programs and policies that favor the strengthening and compliance with national and international regulations.
