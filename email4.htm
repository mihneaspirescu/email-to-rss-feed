Argentina · Internet & OTT

Yeap! to invest US$30 million to deploy LORA network and set up business nationwide

In mid 2017 began the development of the network for IoT, that already has 50 sites in the City of Buenos Aires, Great Buenos Aires, Cordoba, Rosario and Santa Fe. By the end of 2018, national deployment would be completed, as Martin Rajman, CEO of Yeap !, told Convergencialatina, in the framework of the IoT Business Round organized by Cabase last week in La Plata. The firm of national capitals seeks to move forward with the laying - which will reach 1,500 sites - and, at the same time, to promote business (from the agro and banking to monitoring of electric and gas companies, among others). In Uruguay, Yeap! will deploy 25 sites in Montevideo, Pocitos and other locations to the east; and in Paraguay, 35 sites in a first stage, for Asuncion, Encarnación and Ciudad del Este.

Enacom approves merger by absorption of Personnel, Nortel and Telecom by Telecom Argentina

It was last Friday through Resolution 2017-4545 of the nte Nacional de Comunicaciones, according to the Comisión Nacional de Valores (CNV) (National Securities Commission). Telecom Argentina will be in a position to undertake the operations of the absorbed companies as of December 1. The records of the Mobile Telephony Services, Cellular Radio Communications, Personal Communications Area I, II, III and Advanced Mobile Communications, resources, permits and frequencies will be transferred to the National Securities Commission. "Only the completion of the conditioning process by Telecom Argentina of its technical and operational systems is pending to undertake the operations and activities of Telecom Personal, Nortel and Sofora", announces the statement.

Brasil · Regulation · Operators

Anatel sets guidelines and afterwards to analyze Oi's PSA

The board of directors of Anatel approved yesterday, in a new precautionary measure in relation to Oi's Plan Support Agreement (PSA). The regulator found problems in the second proposal presented by the operator, but instead of prohibiting its completion and demanding a new version established guidelines of what the PSA should contemplate. For Anatel it is not admissible that a contribution of capital in a company be linked to the possibility of intervention thereof. It should be remembered that one of the conditions proposed by Oi in its new PSA for the capital contribution of shareholders is precisely that the likelihood of intervening be eliminated. According to Anatel, clauses that compromise the operator's cash, such as the requirement of a prior deposit of guarantees or fees by the company, will not be accepted. What the regulator will do is to analyze afterwards any document that is signed with the creditors and shareholders in the process of OI economic assistance.

Source: Teletime

Brasil · Regulation · Operators

Anatel's audit recommends review of cities contemplated in Telefónica TAC

For Anatel audit, the list of cities presented by Telefónica to invest in exchange for not paying the fines does not comply with the guidelines of the Term of Adjustment of Conduct Regulation (TAC), or with those required by the Tribunal de Cuentas de la Unión (TCU) (Court of Auditors of the Union) in the judgment. One of the findings of the body, in relation to Telefónica's proposal, is that there is an imbalance based on a selection of a larger percentage of municipalities with more competition than those with less competition.

In addition the audit report mentions the statements of entities and companies, especially small Internet providers, who demand that the list of targeted municipalities meet FTTH expansion commitments. "These questions foundation is relevant when looking to comply with the provisions of the TAC Regulation, especially in the perspective of the efficiency of the public policy of broadband access expansion that must be covered by investment compensation", states the body.

Source: Teletime

SQM to devote 83% of its investments to lithium exploitation

In the next few weeks, the SQM board, led by Eugenio Ponce, will review and approve the 2018-2019 Capex, which includes projects for at least US$485 million for the next two years and where 83% will be used for lithium. The protagonist of the next investments that SQM will execute will be the lithium. Precisely, among the main investment projects in which SQM works are the expansion of the production of lithium carbonate in Chile, which is increasing from 48,000 tonnes to 63,000, with an estimated Capex of US$50 million.

Colombia · Internet & OTT · Software and Applications

Regulation for crowdfunding platforms to come into effect in 2018

The project presented by the Unidad de Regulación Financiera (URF) (Financial Regulation Unit) that was to be ready in September expects comments from the industry to be finished. Felipe Lega, director of the URF, said that the decree will allow to "link people who are looking for attractive investment alternatives and people, like SMEs, who are looking for financing alternatives". The platforms will share the information with the risk centers to guarantee the balance of the system and the risk level. At the moment, US$2,300 investment ceilings for each project and US$233,575 of financing for each of them have been established, although the subject is still being discussed. The platforms will be monitored by the Financial Superintendent, in accordance with the asset laundering rules.

Colombia · Internet & OTT

Cyberlunes generated transactions for US$139.4 million

Payment processing networks reported 694,602 transactions and more than one and a half million visits to Cyberlunes number 11 organized last week by the Cámara Colombiana de Comercio Electrónico (CCCE) (Colombian Chamber of E-Commerce), with the support of the Ministry of Information and Communications Technologies (MinTIC), the Police Cybernetic Center, through the Virtual CAI of the National Police and the Federación Nacional de Comerciantes (Fenalco) (National Federation of Merchants). Site visits increased 172% in relation to the last event held in June 2017. The most consulted segments were fashion (18%); technology (17%); cell phones (14%) and tablets (13%); trips, and sports and fitness (7%). The rest of the categories accounted for 31% of visits.

During the two days, 100 online stores that offered 1,100 products and services participated. Bogotá was the city that made more visits and was followed by Medellín, Cali, Barranquilla and Bucaramanga. Queries from the mobile phone accounted for 65% of the total.

Globales · Internet & OTT

SoftBank Group could buy Uber's shares at 30% of market value

One week after the scandal caused by the disclose of the fact that Uber has hidden for more than a year data robbery of 57 million people worldwide, there is information that the agreement to sell shares for the Japanese group SoftBank can be closed at 30% of market value.

The announcement of the multimillionaire agreement between Uber and the Japanese group Softbank was made on November 13. On November 21, however, it was revealed that hackers stole data from 57 million users and drivers of Uber Technologies, a violation that the company has hidden for more than a year.

According to Bloomberg agency, SofBbank and the group of investors would be negotiating with Uber an investment of more than US$6 billion for a stake that would yield a value for the company of US$48 billion, an amount that is below the market value of the company today, which is US$69 billion. If the agreement is closed, the group would have 14% of Uber and would be one of the main shareholders.

Source: O Globo - Brasil | Original article

México · Regulation · Politics

United States proposal avoids closing NAFTA telecommunications chapter

The United States proposal to re-incorporate the zero rate, to benefit AT&T, prevented negotiators from reaching the goal of closing the telecommunications chapter in the fifth round of negotiations. The United States wants to integrate the telecommunication reform to the NAFTA, including a measure that prevents América Móvil from charging its rivals the communications that end up in its network. "What Mexico would be willing to include (in the NAFTA) is all clauses of the reform being in force", said Senator Gerardo Flores, adding that Mexican negotiators are not willing to review a topic that has already been solved by the Supreme Court of Justice.

Panamá · Software and Applications

90% of Uber's drivers do not comply with decree regulating transport apps

Uber made a test on how its service would be if its complies with the measures established by Decree 331, which will be effective as of next April. The rule implies disconnecting drivers that do not comply with the guidelines, which would be 7,200. The decree establishes that drivers of technological transport platforms (such as Uber and Cabify) must be over 21 years of age and have a Type E1 license, issued by the Traffic and Land Transportation Authority (Spanish: Autoridad de Tránsito y Transporte Terrestre, ATTT) and is limited for payment to be made by e-means.
